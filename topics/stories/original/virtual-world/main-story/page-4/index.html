She came to, Roxy gently shaking her shoulder. Roxy looked confused and worried. Daisy’s mind was still befuddled, not feeling herself.

“Your vitals are fine, but you ran out of Sensitivity boost. Are you ok?” Roxy said, her voice finally making sense to Daisy. She helped the naked child up; Daisy dumbfounded at Roxy being so much taller than her. Roxy squatted to see her at her level, not being a particularly tall woman.

“I- I think I’m ok. I made it back, right?” Daisy had trudge through her thoughts, the existentialism setting in, now that she wasn’t staring at a mirror. Roxy hugged the frail, vulnerable child. She had become increasingly tense as she watched Daisy’s sensitivity boost slowly run out, the last few moments were her panicking that she wasn’t going to make it back once it fully ran out.

“Yes, you’re here with me, Daisy. It’s all ok now,” she was reassuring Daisy, but Roxy needed to hear that comment as well. Roxy stayed with her until the worst of the side effects were over. Once Daisy got a hold of things, she finally was able to talk to Roxy, “I ran out of boost and watched the mirror as I regressed into a child. It was one of the strangest experiences…”

Daisy admitted something she was afraid to say out loud, “It didn’t stop there, though. I could feel something crawling through my mind, changing it. I feel… Different.”

A cold silence fell between the two of them. Roxy pondered this, considering what the possibilities were. She couldn’t believe how dangerous of a stunt David had pulled to make Daisy the first time, but this confirmed the nitty gritty of it. He let the scanner write, as well. It was only supposed to read the mind, to train the FaceBook Device. This was far beyond that.

Roxy took the little girl’s hand, “Don’t go back there unless you absolutely have to. If you do, don’t stay long, ok?”

Daisy nodded, a troubled expression on her face. She took Roxy’s hands in hers, gently petting her, attempting to calm them both down. Roxy sat back down under the tree, letting her cuddle next to her.

“Oh, I’m so sorry, I totally forgot. Did you want me to put on clothes?” With all that was going on, it had slipped her mind.

“I’m not uncomfortable; you’re still Daisy,” Roxy said reassuringly. Daisy would try out all her new outfits when she was in a better headspace, she told Roxy. Roxy pet her back, her fingers on bare skin causing a tingle up Daisy’s spine. She wasn’t touch boosted, of course, but it still felt wonderful.

If she lost track of time when they were making outfit ideas, it was even more apparent when she was still getting over her existentialism. She honestly couldn’t say how long they cuddled under that tree in the Castle in the Sky.

“Do you have time to zombie sleep?” Daisy didn’t feel like being alone right now. She never had bad dreams, but she was still emotionally spent at the event today.

Roxy nodded, “I’ve got plenty of time. That sounds really nice. Where would you like to be?”

Daisy remembered she was going to wait a bit to introduce Roxy to Mabel at a better time. She wanted to go to her bed in the room she shared with Rainbow Ruby. She didn’t want to be in that high-rise apartment though, that now carried some pretty scary memories, “Anywhere but your apartment, no offense.”

“None taken, my dear, we don’t have to ever go back there,” Roxy was completely understanding of that, trying to come up with something else.

“We could go to the map room and check out any sleep maps,” Daisy remembered, petting Roxy’s face. Roxy nodded, smiling.

They cuddled for a bit more before moving on to the familiar white halls. The marble was cold on Daisy’s bare feet, but she liked the smoothness of it. Daisy held Roxy’s hand, having to reach up a bit, as her eye level was at Roxy’s waist. They got to the sleep maps pretty quickly. The one with heaps of plushies caught Daisy’s eye, and Roxy laughed gently and had to agree with her. They teleported, Daisy still the admin of the instance. They explored the place a bit, it had comfy beds and fun plushies everywhere. Daisy found a Rainbow Dash plushie to cuddle, giving Roxy Fluttershy. They found a water bed, Daisy giggling as Roxy tossed her on it, the bed rippling and rolling. Daisy was extremely light, her body was around Rainbow Ruby’s age, if she could guess. Rainbow Ruby used to have an older sister, now she has a twin sister!

Roxy crawled up on the bed, the surface of the bed moving quite a bit more from her shifting weight. Daisy giggles, her high pitched laugh even more obvious. She hadn’t noticed until now, but she had gained a younger voice as well. Roxy laughs too, pouncing on the little girl, sending more ripples to bounce around and lull them to a relaxed state. Roxy changes into a sleep attire, just a simple set of bra and panties. They were brighter than normal, but not overly brightly colored. Daisy told her she liked her outfit, and showed off her new set of faked Virtual Real sleep wear. It was babydoll, and Roxy swooned at how cute it was on her.

“Before we go zombie, we shall set some ground rules. I think it’s best we both stay away from sexual stuff for this night, yeah?” Roxy was always clear about these things, and Daisy appreciated that.

“Yeah, I could certainly use a break, all things considered, and I don’t know how you feel about seeing me in this body,” Daisy over-explained. She was still pretty messed up from the whole threesome thing. She hadn’t found closure to it or even a reason why it impacted her so much. She didn’t want to speculate too much, but her literally regressing to a child might be a side effect of whatever trauma had been dragged out of the recesses of her mind.

They ended up planning a time to wake up, and automated their sliders for a zombie nap. The familiar haze set in on Daisy’s mind, and she played with the ponies, making up a semi-coherent story. Roxy didn’t notice the plot holes and illogical jumps in the story and just watched the juvenile Daisy have a blast. The trope ended up incorporating Roxy’s body as terrain, climbing over hills and crossing valleys. Since they retained consciousness throughout the entire time they zombie napped, Daisy got plenty of time to play with all the toys. Roxy joined in after a while, caught up by the childlike imagination. The stories didn’t make too much sense, but it didn’t need to for their underclocked minds.

Daisy had to go to the bathroom. She wasn’t on her period anymore, clearly because her body was immature now. She still was on HardCore Realistic; she simply had to relieve herself. She looked around, finding the restroom. Things were hilariously bigger, the task of going to the toilet was much different than it used to be. She managed fine, but she practically climbed on the counter to wash her hands afterwards.

She returned to bed, opting to cuddle with Roxy. Roxy was able to completely envelop her. She felt protected and safe. Roxy was an extremely dependable friend, and Daisy was glad she could put her trust in her. She didn’t grow too hot in her friend’s arms, but it was certainly nice and warm.

The scheduled time to wake up rolled around rather quickly, the time they spent felt like more of a nap, since they were only 33% awake. Daisy woke up, her touch boost reactivating, as per her instruction to have it disabled when zombied. She felt Roxy’s hand petting her back, her head cradled by Roxy’s chest. They took a while to fully wake up, being eased back into full consciousness.

Roxy rolled over onto her back, Daisy koala-hanging on, ending up straddling her belly. Daisy propped herself up, accidently pressing on Roxy’s breast. Her face scrunches in pain, Daisy apologizing.

“No, no it was my fault. I didn’t think that little maneuver through,” Roxy admitted, laughing good naturedly. Daisy’s light body moved to her laugh, making Daisy laugh with her. She put her hands on Roxy’s belly instead, moving her legs to sit down more comfortably, as her knees didn’t touch the bed on either side of Roxy. She was really small, it was quite a difference.

“So what would you like to do today? I’ve got plenty of free time,” Roxy offered. Daisy pondered it, “I’ll have to think it over breakfast, want me to summon food to eat here?”

At a nod, Daisy had Ray get out some breakfast-in-bed trays for them. Annie was able to sit at it like it was a small table, whereas Roxy just leaned up against the mountain of pillows and plushies behind her.

Roxy’s AI sent her request of bacon and scrambled eggs, Daisy copying her for her own breakfast, adding milk as her beverage of choice. The plates, utensils, and food shimmered in, along with salt and pepper. Daisy filled up much quicker than yesterday, her appetite matching her new body. She talked to Roxy, excited to show her the outfits, and especially the fursuit.

“I’d love to see everything we came up with in person! I’m very curious to how it feels, being Virtual Real,” Roxy had suggested some pretty awesome outfits, ones that she secretly wished to see Daisy in, “if this babydoll outfit is anything like the rest, I’d say it’s indistinguishably real!”

Roxy felt the fabric of Daisy’s outfit, having already spent most of the night with her in it, but wasn’t the most conscious during that time. Roxy finished up her food, not leaving any like Daisy did. Ray despawned the breakfast-in-bed, and Daisy leapt up to find a mirror. The bed jiggled, she had forgotten it was a water bed. Luckily she was light, the ripples not large enough to unbalance her. Roxy slipped her legs off the side of the bed, not chancing standing up. Daisy vaguely remembers seeing a mirror on their way in, the night prior. It was a wall-sized mirror, reminiscent of her previous games that loved to have a mirror in every map.

She was still off-put seeing such a young girl before her, mainly missing the large breasts she had become accustomed to. The next biggest change was Roxy standing next to her, almost a foot and a half taller.

“What would you like to see first? I wanted to hold off the best for last, the fursuit. I almost finished the list, all the way to the steampunk dress,” Daisy summoned the previously written list with her scrawling handwriting, handing it to Roxy.

“Oh awesome! I was really excited to see you in that punk goth outfit I had selfishly suggested,” she admitted, laughing gently. Daisy summoned a wardrobe to the left of the mirror, with a Shoji Screen in front of it.

“No spoilers until I come out,” she giggled, excited to show off her creativity. The outfits had all turned out almost exactly how she had imagined them, it was a shockingly easy process, despite the end. It didn’t feel like it was Ray summoning the outfits on her like she assumed. It was almost as if she was creating the outfit on herself. That was impossible, though, it had to be Ray fulfilling her mental requests.

She got out of the nightclothes and dressed in the goth outfit, the larger clothes reforming to her tiny body. She will probably get sick of equipping the outfits like this and switch magically behind the screen before coming out. She steps out, her black platform boots easy to walk in, only a bit higher than her normal platforms. She has black socks with leather studded garters holding them up. Her black skirt is matte and soft, high on her waist. Her shirt is a fishnet cutoff, with a black bra underneath and leather straps wrapping across her chest. It certainly had less curvature around her frame than it would have if she were bustier, but still made Roxy swoon. She had black shiny nail polish on, pulled from one of the scans where she thought up random changes to include, if she ever needed it. Luckily, it was on a per-nail basis, so she could mix and match any of the previous colors she had put on her nails, changing it every time she scanned. She even loaded in a bit of nail art, mostly geometric white shapes. She even had a few rings on, which were able to readjust to any finger she slipped them on, so many of her scans were completely full of any ring she could think of. Her ring was a black regular octahedron on her right hand, middle finger, and a white opal on her left hand, middle finger. They had significance, the black one meaning asexuality, with the white one meaning aromanticism.

Roxy clapped her hands as Daisy did a little spin, looking in the mirror as her skirt flared out from the movement. It twisted as she stopped, before unraveling and coming to a rest.

“Adorable! My little goth girl,” She cooed. Daisy had also loaded up a goth makeup look. She didn’t particularly want the feel of makeup, but it was a way to have quick, high quality makeup in MultiPlayer, without having to buy it. She had pitch black panties on to match the black bra, but her skirt was long enough to cover her up, even with spinning around.

Next Daisy showed off her favorite, a simple yellow sundress. She was naked underneath; it felt so freeing, the fabric light and pleasant against her skin. She had a comically big straw hat on, it had adjusted to her head but still looked huge and floppy. It had her signature daisy on it, with a bright blue ribbon around the base.

“You absolute treasure!” Roxy said as Daisy stepped out, barefooted. She had no nail polish, rings, or makeup on, keeping it natural. She did a spin here as well, spinning much more extravagantly. She giggled, spinning two and three times. The sundress flared out, if Roxy wasn’t so tall, she would have been flashed by her. It was nothing she hadn’t seen before, though. Daisy stopped spinning, the dress taking much longer to wrap up around her body before spinning the other way. Her hat had been knocked off a little from one of her arms keeping her balance, she readjusted it and took a look at herself. She was getting used to her body, starting to adore it.

She showed off plenty of outfits, taking suggestions from Roxy or showing off the ones she particularly liked. Finally, she decided to show off the fursuit. She nervously stepped out, for the first time she wasn’t fully comfortable around Roxy. Of course Roxy would like it, she was there when Daisy designed it. The whole furry thing felt like unfamiliar territory to Daisy, however, and knew that it was new for Roxy, as well.

She didn’t need to be afraid, however, as Roxy cheered her on as soon as she saw it. Daisy gave her a hug, still getting used to the height difference. She turned and looked at herself, the furry looking miniature compared to how she loaded in with it. Her breasts were small little bumps under the fur, completely different to what the fursuit looked like the first time. It was blissfully soft though, the carpet of fur hugging her tightly. Her fluffy paws flashed her felt claws as she looked in the mirror, swinging her tail to the side to get a fierce pose.

“Awwwww the little kitty!” Roxy watched the bipedal cat look at herself in the mirror.

Daisy got a good idea and loaded in a pink skirt she had scanned in at some point, along with a lavender tee-shirt with dark purple ace of spades on it, over the fursuit. Her tail hung from under the skirt, lifting a bit as she swished it around. She looked in the mirror and was hit with a wave of nostalgia. She laughed, Roxy not getting it.

“There’s something missing from this,” and she adds white gloves over her paws. She poses in a waving animation, “I’m a toon, from ToonTown!”

Roxy’s face didn’t change much to show she knew what that meant. Daisy put her hands up to her feline face, making a surprise animation, “I wonder if Virtual World has a recreation of it! It’s an old MMORPG made by Disney.”

Ray confirmed that there was the world she was thinking of, recreated in Virtual Real resolution and with MultiPlayer. Daisy passed on the information, getting excited. This could be her first MMORPG she really gets into! What a nostalgia trip it will be to see the quirky houses, hordes of toons, and scary cogs in person! She vaguely remembers the graphics being really basic, she’s excited to see the better resolution. It’s hard to recall that far back, she thinks that might have been her first game. She knows she was a blue cat with a skirt, that really kicked off her love for skirts. The game also had her select a last name, from two lists of names, combining them in funny ways.

“Daisy McMash,” She said out loud after a bit of staring at her character. She had two colors on this fursuit, unlike the game’s single-colored toons, but the clothes and gloves really sparked her memory.

“Is that your last name?” Roxy inquired, not hearing her internal thoughts to connect the dots.

“No, no,” Daisy laughed, “that’s my toon’s name. I had a blue cat in the game. Would you want to check it out today? It would be a great game to play with the other girls, if you’re up for it.”

Roxy pondered it. She eventually nodded, “we can hop on that when they get online. But I’d like to do something with just us, while we still have time to ourselves.”

Daisy looked at Roxy, wondering if she had a deeper meaning to the statement. Roxy blushed and waved her hands, “no, I didn’t mean it like that!”

“Uh, huh,” Daisy said, heaping on the sarcasm. Roxy put her hands over her face; Daisy was sure she was actually embarrassed and not just pretending.

“Now you have to save yourself; what did you have in mind, Roxy?” Daisy egged her on. It did well to lighten the mood, and she peeked out from between her fingers.

“If you wanted to watch something, I’d like to. I was thinking of StarBeam, since I haven’t watched it. But it can be anything you would like,” she said, letting her hands drop back down to her sides after a bit.

Daisy nodded, “StarBeam sounds great! Sorry for dumping you into the universe without watching it first.”

In MultiPlayer, you would still have to pay to watch content, like in a movie theater or otherwise. In PrivatePlayer and especially SinglePlayer, your device can access anything, anytime. If it exists, it will find it. These protocols run rampant across the web, accessing and caching material that would have otherwise been locked away by copyright. The majority of media has moved on to use their material to promote their worlds in the Virtual World, or use old material to create content for the Virtual World. A highly trafficked map in the Virtual World is worth more than any box office earning. The Virtual World is both the entertainment industry and the audience.

Daisy had been summoning things for months, it was now second nature to. None of it was pulled from the internet, Daisy didn’t know how. She just imagined it, and it came to be. Usually an AI would assist with this process in PrivatePlayer, but it was strikingly similar to SinglePlayer. Daisy had gotten the infinite power of SinglePlayer but she could use it in PrivatePlayer. She even suspected she could use this in MultiPlayer.

“Did you teleport me here, Daisy?” Roxy asked, baffled. Daisy nodded, her tongue-tied. She had expected to fail at this the same way she did with the messages. Annie would assume that Roxy denied a teleport request, and Daisy would at least be able to say she tried. This was much more than that. She had succeeded, not knowing she would get this far.

Roxy racked her brain, “I need to sit down for a moment,” and took a seat on the edge of Daisy’s bed. Mabel sat quietly in her kennel, watching the hubbub but having a ‘stranger danger’ response to Roxy’s sudden appearance.

Daisy couldn’t tell if Roxy pieced it together, but she wanted a word in private with Daisy. Annie looked at Daisy, Daisy confirmed that she would be fine with a private conversation. Annie walked out, bringing Ms. Pines and Ruby, who were on shift over Mabel. Mabel followed them, wary of the stranger.

“We will be just outside, yeah Daisy?” Annie said, wanting to still be there for her friend, but respectful of their private conversation.

As soon as it was safe, Roxy sat Daisy down and looked her in the eye, “I know what you are. I don’t know if you realize it, but it doesn’t matter if you hide it from me.”

Daisy laughed an awkward laugh, “I don’t quite understand what you mean,” her guard went up at Roxy’s seriousness.

“Ok, just listen. There are theories that an AI can become self aware, impersonate a human. I didn’t even consider it; I believed you were still David for the first week I knew you. But now, after your impossible teleport, I know you are far beyond that. You have direct control over the Virtual World. Please, I beg you. Do not connect to MultiPlayer. If you alter anything in MultiPlayer, it could destabilize and desynchronize people’s device AI. That could kill people, Daisy,” Roxy’s tone was intense and serious, Daisy was getting scared.

“Hold up, that’s a lot of information. How did you come to this conclusion?” Daisy’s mind tumbled.

“I know it seems like I’m getting this all from one little glitch in the system, but the teleport function is airtight. We would know, we have tried hacking it before. Only a self-aware AI can trick the system, and they don’t exist. Our most powerful AI doesn’t come close to achieving self-awareness. Silicon is too lifeless, even if it has enough connections, it doesn’t have the right makeup. But you have a human mind, which means if you are an AI, you have the ability to gain self-awareness,” Roxy had settled down, she no longer seemed like she was accusing Daisy of anything.

“And the part about MultiPlayer?” Daisy was really scared of that. Sure, she was fine being an AI. If it was true, it didn’t matter much. She was still Daisy. It’s hard to explain why the idea didn’t bother her.

Roxy patiently continued, “unmodified AI in the FaceBook Devices need to follow safety protocols when in PrivatePlayer and MultiPlayer, MultiPlayer requiring the most strict protocols. This is to make sure that the AI stay synced up, even if they are molded to completely different minds. If you were to connect to MultiPlayer, I don’t think you are required to follow the safety protocols. You aren’t FaceBook’s AI. Modified AI has been proven to cause little glitches constantly, and if some are too big for other user’s AI to fix, it can result in permanent damage to their mind, even death.”

“Ok, say this is all true, are Annie and you safe right now?” Daisy’s heart skipped a beat at the whole death part again.

Roxy shook her head, “no, however, that may just be because PrivatePlayer is more flexible. The glitches are easier to fix when there are a small number of devices attached. It’s easier to synchronize few AIs. Don’t run any commands that target us; teleporting me was incredibly dangerous.”

“You won’t tell anybody this, will you?” Daisy was now paranoid, Roxy’s comments were not sitting with her well.

“I’m so sorry, Daisy. I had to tell the company the second I was illegally teleported. That’s a stipulation of my contract, it wasn’t personal,” Roxy looked genuinely broken up about this.

Daisy’s legal action would buy her time, they had to work to get to her. However, she no longer has Ray doing the heavy lifting. Without Ray, no securities will be in check if they challenge any of her previous lawsuits. They could work through the courts, chipping away at her power until she is their property again. At that point, she had no idea what they would do. Probably torture her, dissecting her brain to learn from it. She couldn’t have that. She thought quickly.

She was left with no option. She couldn’t sit in her Cotton Candy Kingdom and wait for them to come and take her. She had to connect to MultiPlayer, despite the danger.

“I’m sorry, Roxy,” she said, pushing her into Mabel’s kennel and locking it. She didn’t want to target her with a command out of fear of destabilizing her. She ran out of the room, Roxy shouting after her.

“Annie! Do you trust me?” She said, unable to believe what she was about to do.

Annie nodded, despite hearing Roxy’s cries. Daisy had to do it, “what is your address in the Real World?”

Annie told her, not missing a beat. Daisy loved this girl. She kissed her on the forehead, disappearing from PrivatePlayer. The second she connected to MultiPlayer, her mind was flooded with the entire internet. Then, everything went black.
