This caught Daisy off guard. Annie can scan for hours at a time. Maybe just on big events such as her birthday, but still. Importing so many outfits must be expensive. Daisy felt one of the many leaf-like extensions off the rim of the skirt, “you’re like a little fairy!”

The dress was strapless, hemmed with short white frills. Down the center of the bright green dress was dark green lines of runic design, passing over the underlayers of black fabric on her tummy. Her waist had an inner skirt topped with white, fading into a bright green by the edge of it. It ended in a leaf-like edge, the surface of the underskirt had big teal spots scattered across it. Where the inner skirt was tight to her body, like a pencil skirt, the rest of the skirt was as floofy and extravagant as you can get. The upper bodice extended into an outer skirt, flared out from all the material under it. Under it was the leaf-like petticoat, the fabric Daisy was feeling. It was teal near the top where it came out from under the overskirt, and transitioned into bright green by the tips of the large leaves. Her dress had 3 tails that ended in a peacock-style leaf, flared out and with bright blue designs in the center of it, forming an eye. They were so long they dragged on the ground like a train. Finally, the front of her dress, where the mint green bodice opened up to show off her inner skirt, was boarded by 2 long strips of fabric that almost touched the ground in front of her. They faded from green to blue at the tips, their arrow-like features complimented by intricate and symmetrical designs in the same dark green lines that were at the edges of the bodice and decorating the front of the chest and belly.

Annie had Inko put her hair back up in her signature curly twintails, high up on her head. She put a teal bow around her neck, that had a pin clip on the center of the tied ribbon. The pin had a rhombus shaped gem, boarded by gold with 3 leaves on each side, pointing outwards like wings. The ribbon even had gold tipped ends, with intricate designs etched into the surface of the caps. The teal ribbon held a collar of frills to her neck, making for a very extravagant… choker? Daisy didn’t know enough about fashion to know what it was called, but it looked adorable. Upon closer inspection, mostly because she was interested in it, she noticed that the collar had groups of four tear-drop teal decorations, in a plus sign arrangement. They repeated along the edge of the white frills, complimenting the teal ribbon that held it all together.

Annie slipped on some high heeled boots, they were silver around the foot, but had a dark green metal cover the silver past the ankle. It ended just below the knee, the metal edge shaped uniquely, gold running along the edges. The edge dipped down in the front, making an arrow pointing downwards, showing the skin above her shin. Lastly, Annie put some gloves on that also ended in leaf-like tapers at the elbow.

“You look absolutely adorable, Annie!” Daisy complimented her.

Annie blushed, hugging Daisy, “thank you.”

Daisy’s arm looped around her small shoulders, placing her hand on her bare skin. This girl was something else, but she really warmed up to her. Daisy was glad for it though, they had so much in common. She was a great friend. Wait a second, how long had she been connected?

“Hey, don’t worry if you need to get off and get something to eat. I can wait for you to take care of yourself before we head out,” Daisy realized this was an extremely long time for someone to be connected off of life support.

“Want to wait for me at your house in PrivatePlayer, and I’ll send a request when I’m finished up? Thank you for thinking of me, I’m kinda embarrassed to have neglected myself for so long,” Annie admitted, though she seemed happy.

Daisy told her she would see her then, and didn’t wait for the hug to release before she disappeared off to her PrivatePlayer instance of her room. She could play with Ruby while she waits!

Ray must have notified the NPC of Daisy’s plan, for it didn’t take long for the quiet tap on the door as Ruby knocks and opens it. Daisy spawned a Ruby-sized bed in the corner of the room before she forgot, and turned to the little girl, “Good morning Ruby! Do you want to play while we wait for my friend?”

She ruffled the little girl’s head as she hugged her around the waist. Time passed pretty quickly, the teleport request not taking long to speak to her from everywhere and nowhere. Annie materialized outside the room, entering to see Daisy playing with Ruby.

“Aww, lookit you two,” Annie thought it was great that Daisy could play with Ruby anytime, “It’s like you’re sisters!”

“Yeah, I even put her bed back so we can be roommates, huh Ruby?” Daisy smiled at the girl. She, being an obedient NPC, didn’t mind in the slightest, excitedly nodding. Annie didn’t mind joining them to play for a bit, before they headed out to the Virtual Market.

Daisy let Annie go first, so she could teleport to her instance. If they both tried joining at the same time, there were no guarantees they would spawn in the same instance, nor the same location. It was just easier to use the teleport feature.

Daisy didn’t know what to expect, but when she arrived, it was much bigger than she thought. From where she stood, she could see floating planets and worlds above her. Annie explained that these were actually part of the Virtual Market. The market was themed so if you wanted fantasy add-ons, you would actually be in a fantasy themed map, and likewise with anything genre you can think of. The worlds were massive, various stands with NPCs at the booths, or some shops having their own storefront with a real person working at it. Individual sellers, much like a convention, could set up their own stand and sell their wares. It was like the largest convention Daisy had ever been to, with entire worlds for the individual themes. Daisy mentioned this to Annie, and she basically confirmed it. Cosplay groups would attend this like it were a convention, and there were even performers scheduled throughout the day. A non-stop convention. What a dream. And tickets were free, because you’d likely spend all your money on merchandise, anyway.

This certainly confirmed why there were exclusives. That was a huge part of a convention. The sellers probably had to sign a contract of how much they could sell, the bigger sellers pushing the quantity over quality. They were like wholesale, though, not providing the highly sought after exclusives that happened in secret among the smaller sellers. Daisy doubted that she would find anything exclusive, but she was going for something simple, like a tattoo. She had a huge bonus still sitting in her account, if she did find anything special.

Oh, that’s a pleasant flowery smell. She would activate her Scent boost, but that seems a bit dangerous in a store with this many smells drifting around.
